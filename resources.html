Resource & Cost Management

Human, physical, financial resources; budgeting and cost control

📘 PMBOK

PMBOK provides structured processes for resource estimation, acquisition, team development, cost baselines, and control, integrated with scheduling and risk management.

📗 PRINCE2

PRINCE2 manages resources through planning and stage control, assigning responsibilities via product-based structures and setting tolerances for cost deviations.

📙 ISO

ISO emphasizes identifying, allocating, and controlling physical, human, and financial resources in line with governance and project objectives, supported by cost monitoring.

✅ Similarities

  • All standards require structured resource planning and cost control.
  • Resources are integrated with scheduling, risk, and scope baselines.

⚖️ Differences

  • PMBOK is most detailed on cost baselines; PRINCE2 uses tolerances and roles; ISO ties resource control closely to governance structures.
