Risk & Issue Management

Identification, assessment, response planning, and issue escalation

📘 PMBOK

Provides a dedicated knowledge area for risk (planning, identification, analysis, response, monitoring). Issues are managed through escalation paths, integrated into monitoring and controlling processes.

📗 PRINCE2

Uses a Risk Theme with a Risk Register and Issue Register. Issues are categorized (change, off-specification, problem/concern) and escalated through formal controls and tolerances.

📙 ISO

ISO 21502 emphasizes identifying, analyzing, and treating risks at project and program levels. Issues are deviations requiring corrective action and are documented for organizational learning.

✅ Similarities

  • All recognize risks and issues as separate but related concepts.
  • Each requires structured documentation and escalation.

⚖️ Differences

  • PMBOK = knowledge area focus; PRINCE2 = risk/issue registers and tolerances; ISO = high-level governance guidance.
