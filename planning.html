Planning Approaches & Plan Hierarchy

Product-based planning, progressive elaboration, planning levels across standards

📘 PMBOK

PMBOK emphasizes progressive elaboration and rolling wave planning, with plans structured at multiple levels—strategic, project, stage, and iteration—depending on development approach.

📗 PRINCE2

PRINCE2 uses product-based planning at each stage. It clearly defines the hierarchy of plans: project plan, stage plans, team plans, and optional exception plans, all driven by product breakdown structures.

📙 ISO

ISO 21502 outlines structured planning at project and phase levels, recommending consistent plan hierarchies linked to organizational governance and development approaches.

✅ Similarities

  • All three recognize multiple planning levels and iterative refinement.
  • Planning integrates deliverables, activities, resources, and controls.

⚖️ Differences

  • PRINCE2 focuses on product-based hierarchy; PMBOK emphasizes progressive elaboration; ISO connects planning hierarchy with governance.
