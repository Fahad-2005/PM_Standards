Scope & Deliverables Definition

Product breakdowns, deliverables, baselines, and requirements control

📘 PMBOK

PMBOK treats scope as a core domain—defining scope baselines, WBS, requirements traceability, and formal change control to manage deliverables and expectations.

📗 PRINCE2

PRINCE2 uses product breakdown structures to define scope in terms of products and their quality criteria. Scope changes are controlled through issue and change management procedures.

📙 ISO

ISO emphasizes defining deliverables and boundaries early, supported by structured documentation, approvals, and change procedures to maintain baseline integrity.

✅ Similarities

  • All standards require clear definition and control of deliverables.
  • Scope baselines are maintained through formal change management.

⚖️ Differences

  • PRINCE2 frames scope around products; PMBOK focuses on requirements and WBS; ISO highlights boundary definition and governance linkage.
